Cool Minerals

These are minerals that I personally find really interesting due to certain properties such as their natural shape or color. Click on an image to reveal a cool fact!

Alexandrite

"Emerald by day, ruby by night." Alexandrite exhibits a fascinating color change phenomenon, appearing green in daylight and red under incandescent light.

Benitoite

Benitoite is California's official state gem and is renowned for its striking blue color that almost resembles a blue sapphire.

Cumengeite

Cumengeite is a rare copper mineral known naturally found in a six-pointed star shape. Deposits are incredibly tiny, smaller than the tip of a Q-tip.

Bismuth

Bismuth in its raw form is a dull grey metal. However, it melts at low temperatures and upon cooling, forms shiny "staircase-shaped" crystals.

Rhodochrosite

This mineral is prized for its beautiful pink to rose-red color and is often used in jewelry. It is commonly found in its opaque form but transparent crystals are worth thousands of dollars.

Titanite

Also known as sphene, titanite exhibits a strong trichroism (difference in color when viewed along three axes) and has a stronger dispersive index than diamond.
